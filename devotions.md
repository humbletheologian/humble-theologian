Devotions create space to slow down, attend to Scripture and become more aware of God’s presence in ordinary life.

This collection brings together daily readings from different voices and traditions. Some are brief and practical. Others invite slower reflection. Each series can be followed from beginning to end or opened whenever you need encouragement.

## Love as a Verb 365

**A year of short reflections on living out Christian love.**

*Love as a Verb 365* explores what it means to make love visible through everyday choices, relationships and acts of faithfulness. Each devotion offers a brief reading and a practical invitation to put love into action.

[Read Love as a Verb 365](/love-as-a-verb/)

## Spurgeon: Morning and Evening

**Classic daily readings from Charles Haddon Spurgeon.**

*Morning and Evening* has encouraged Christians for generations. Each day includes a morning and an evening reading centred on Scripture, trust in God and life with Christ.

The original work is in the public domain. The language has been retained as much as possible while presenting the readings in an accessible online format.

[Read Spurgeon: Morning and Evening](/spurgeon/)

## Ordinary Faithfulness

**Reflections on following Jesus in the routines and responsibilities of everyday life.**

Christian discipleship is often formed through small, repeated acts rather than dramatic moments. *Ordinary Faithfulness* considers prayer, work, relationships, perseverance and the quiet ways God shapes us over time.

[Read Ordinary Faithfulness](/ordinary-faithfulness/)

## Using These Devotions

There is no single correct way to use a devotional. You might:

- read one entry at the beginning or end of the day
- pause over the Scripture passage before reading the reflection
- write down a sentence, question or prayer
- share a reading with a friend, family member or small group
- return to a devotion that speaks into a particular season

A devotion is not a replacement for reading Scripture. It is a companion that can help us listen, reflect, pray and respond.
