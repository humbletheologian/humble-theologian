**Source author:** Pauline Sheehan

April 24

Too Harried to Be Sacred This is a sacred day before our Lord. —Nehemiah 8:10

One harried day, I stopped for fast food and ran into an elderly man from my church. He pulled his son toward me. “I want you to meet Pauline, she’s a parish nurse,” he told his son. Then he explained to me, “We’re on our way back to the nursing home. My wife . . . is slipping away. If you could . . . it would be nice . . . come see her.” What did I get myself into? I groaned inwardly. God, you know all the plans I had for today… After all, I wasn’t going to start the parish nurse position until the first of the year. Another thought flashed through my mind. Let’s see if you really want to be a parish nurse. Okay, God, I’ll go there and leave my uprooted schedule for you to handle, I responded to Him. I went. I did nothing—sat, listened, watched the husband. He talked to his wife, kissed her, and wiped her face. He appreciated my visit so much.

When we show love, we make any day a sacred day. — Pauline Sheehan —
