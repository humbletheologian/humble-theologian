# Humble Theologian Content Guide

## Article length

Major articles should normally aim for **1,800 to 2,200 words**. Some topics may need more, but the usual target is about 2,000 words.

## Audience

Write for **Bible college students and serious theologically minded Christians**. Assume readers want depth, but do not assume they know every technical term.

## Voice

- humble, pastoral and thoughtful
- serious but accessible
- convictional without being combative
- clear enough for serious lay Christians
- deep enough to help Bible college students

## Standard major article structure

1. Opening question or pastoral issue
2. Why this matters
3. Biblical and theological background
4. Main views or interpretive options
5. Where I currently stand, if relevant
6. What I still wrestle with
7. Why it matters for discipleship, pastoral care or church life
8. Further reading

## Length guide

- Theology guides: 1,800–2,500 words
- Personal theological position articles: 1,500–2,200 words
- Disability / pastoral care articles: 1,800–2,500 words
- Bible college help articles: 1,200–2,000 words
- Book notes: 300–700 words
- Love as a Verb devotionals: 500–900 words
- Spurgeon reflections: 500–1,000 words

## Copyright rule

Do not upload or republish full copyrighted books or modern devotional texts. Published books should become recommended reading entries using your own summaries and reflections.


## Typography sizing rule

Use even whole-number pixel font sizes only. Avoid odd values and fractional/rem-derived sizes such as 17px or 21.6px. The current design uses 12px, 14px, 16px, 18px, 20px, 22px, 28px, 32px, 40px and 56px.

## Launch content rule

Avoid publishing placeholder pages. If a section is not ready, either leave it out of the menu or publish a real introductory page that clearly explains the purpose of the section. The launch version should feel substantial from day one.


## Launch article length check

Major public theology articles should normally sit around 1,800-2,200 words. The current launch position articles have been expanded to this range and are not to be treated as short placeholders. Shorter content types such as devotionals, book notes and classic devotional texts do not need to meet the 2,000-word standard.
