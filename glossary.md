# Glossary

This glossary is here to help newer Christians, Bible college students and thoughtful readers keep moving when an unfamiliar word appears. The definitions are deliberately brief. They are not meant to settle every debate, but to give enough meaning to keep reading carefully.

<div class="glossary-list">

<section class="glossary-entry" id="annihilationism">
<h2>Annihilationism</h2>
<p>The view that final judgment results in the destruction of the wicked rather than endless conscious torment. It is sometimes called conditional immortality because eternal life is understood as God’s gift, not an automatic property of every human soul.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: hell, final judgment, eternal life.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="atonement">
<h2>Atonement</h2>
<p>What God does through the life, death and resurrection of Jesus to deal with sin, reconcile humanity to himself and defeat evil. Christians use several biblical images for this, including sacrifice, victory, redemption, reconciliation and substitution.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: cross, reconciliation, salvation.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="canon">
<h2>Canon</h2>
<p>The recognised collection of writings received by the church as Holy Scripture. When Christians speak of the biblical canon, they mean the books that belong to the Bible.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: Scripture, Old Testament, New Testament.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="christus-victor">
<h2>Christus Victor</h2>
<p>A way of describing the atonement that emphasises Christ’s victory over sin, death, evil and the powers that enslave creation.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: atonement, resurrection, kingdom of God.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="creed">
<h2>Creed</h2>
<p>A short summary of core Christian belief, often used in worship, teaching and baptismal instruction. The Apostles’ Creed and Nicene Creed are classic examples.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: confession, statement of faith, doctrine.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="confession">
<h2>Confession</h2>
<p>A fuller statement of Christian doctrine, often associated with a denomination or theological tradition. Confessions usually say more than the basic creeds and may address disputed issues in greater detail.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: creed, statement of faith.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="dispensationalism">
<h2>Dispensationalism</h2>
<p>A theological framework that often emphasises distinct periods in God’s dealings with humanity and a strong distinction between Israel and the church. Popular forms are commonly associated with premillennialism and a pre-tribulation rapture, though dispensationalists do not all agree on every detail.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: eschatology, covenant theology, rapture.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="eschatology">
<h2>Eschatology</h2>
<p>The area of theology concerned with last things, including resurrection, judgment, the return of Christ, final hope and new creation.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: resurrection, new creation, millennium.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="exegesis">
<h2>Exegesis</h2>
<p>The careful interpretation of a biblical text in its context. Exegesis aims to draw meaning from the text rather than read our assumptions into it.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: hermeneutics, context, interpretation.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="hermeneutics">
<h2>Hermeneutics</h2>
<p>The study of interpretation. In Bible reading, hermeneutics asks how we understand Scripture faithfully, including genre, context, culture, language and application.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: exegesis, genre, context.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="image-of-god">
<h2>Image of God</h2>
<p>The biblical teaching that human beings are created with a God-given dignity and vocation. Human worth is not based on intelligence, productivity, independence, physical ability or social usefulness.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: dignity, disability theology, creation.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="justification">
<h2>Justification</h2>
<p>God’s gracious act of declaring people right with himself through faith in Christ. It is not a claim that humans have earned righteousness, but that they are accepted because of God’s saving work in Jesus.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: grace, faith, salvation.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="kingdom-of-god">
<h2>Kingdom of God</h2>
<p>God’s reign and rule, announced and embodied in Jesus and awaiting final fulfilment in new creation. It is not merely heaven, politics or personal morality.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: gospel, new creation, discipleship.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="new-creation">
<h2>New Creation</h2>
<p>The biblical hope that God will renew creation, raise the dead and dwell with his people. Christian hope is not escape from creation, but the healing and renewal of all things in Christ.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: resurrection, eternal life, kingdom of God.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="penal-substitution">
<h2>Penal Substitution</h2>
<p>An atonement model that emphasises Christ bearing judgment in the place of sinners. It is one way Christians explain how the cross deals with guilt, sin and justice.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: atonement, cross, forgiveness.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="rapture">
<h2>Rapture</h2>
<p>A term used for believers being caught up to meet Christ. Christians disagree over whether this is a separate event before a period of tribulation or part of the one public return of Christ.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: eschatology, second coming, tribulation.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="sanctification">
<h2>Sanctification</h2>
<p>The work of God by which believers are made holy and shaped into the likeness of Christ. It involves grace, obedience, formation, repentance and the work of the Spirit.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: discipleship, holiness, formation.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="soteriology">
<h2>Soteriology</h2>
<p>The area of theology concerned with salvation: what God saves us from, how God saves, who is saved and what salvation is for.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: grace, atonement, justification, sanctification.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="statement-of-faith">
<h2>Statement of Faith</h2>
<p>A written summary of what a church, ministry or organisation believes. It may function like a brief confession, especially in churches that do not use historic confessional language.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: creed, confession, doctrine.</p>
</section>

<section class="glossary-entry" id="trinity">
<h2>Trinity</h2>
<p>The Christian confession that the one God is Father, Son and Holy Spirit. Christians do not worship three gods, but the one God eternally known in three persons.</p>
<p class="see-also">See also: Father, Son, Holy Spirit, prayer.</p>
</section>

</div>
